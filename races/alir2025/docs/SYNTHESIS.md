## 4. Case studies (by lesson value)

**1. The finish door** (verified-fact). Boats entering the last 15 nm before 14:00
Friday crossed in 2.2–3.2 h; 14:30–21:30 took 4.5–6.7 h; after midnight ~3.7–4.7 h.
Only Wahoo, Avalanche and Max beat the squall to the line; Max by 80 minutes. Habiru
was the last boat through a *half-open* door (entered 13:15, took 3.17 h — the squall
caught her final three miles). Daffodil entered 2¾ h behind Max and paid 5.78 h.
Nothing about this is a margin anyone sailed for or against — it is arrival time
compounding through weather. *The counterweight, owned honestly: the boats inside
the closed door got the squall's push (C6) and the leaders never needed it.*

**2. The Sound beat — where Daffodil's race went** (verified-fact + model-supported
wind; owner corroborates). TWA 20–58° upwind all Friday morning, breeze 11→7 kt.
Against Habiru on overlapping water: +2:07 elapsed, 6.4 nm more sailed, 9 maneuvers
to 4, made-good 49.9 vs 59.2 nm. An F31R's worst mode, compounded by the extra
tacks. Bounded counterfactual (two assumptions, both stated): Habiru-pace Sound →
door entry ~12:30 → ~3 h door → finish ~15:30–16:00, ≈4.5 h saved, ≈28:30 corrected,
≈5th–8th unified. Even perfect, ~45 min short of Max's door cohort — the first half
had already been sailed as well as the fleet's best.

**3. The squall as a gift** (participant-recollection + verified stations). For the
mid-fleet the squall was violence *and* the only pressure in an otherwise dying
afternoon: Daffodil doused, took ~30 kt for two minutes, and rode the aftermath at
5–6 kt where before there had been 1–3. The overlay ships the occurrence; the door
chart ships the consequence; no per-boat "hit at HH:MM" claims (5-min pings cannot
carry them).

**4. The shore split and the anchor** (testimony + verified geometry). SG hugged
Long Island, Towhee went to Connecticut, 4.5 nm apart mid-Sound; SG won the
division and then spent 55 minutes anchored 175 yards from the line waiting for the
new breeze. In a race that ends in a park, the last mile is a tidal problem, not a
sailing problem.

## 5. Lessons (team-facing cut; specific, non-blaming)

1. **In a Sound finish, arrival time is strategy.** The door swung on a two-hour
   window. Any forecast showing post-frontal high pressure over the western Sound
   at night makes the afternoon the finish line. (From case 1; applies to both
   programs for 2026.)
2. **Know the boat's dead mode and buy out of it early.** The F31R gives back its
   reach gains in light upwind. The Friday-morning choice worth studying isn't any
   single tack — it's whether the corner exit (03:45, with the ebb building) allowed
   a shore/tide line that kept TWA wider. (From case 2; the register keeps the
   owner's memory and the track's account side by side.)
3. **Ratings are the third boat in every duel.** Wahoo beat Max around the course by
   1:49 and lost by 4:50-inside-noise; Daffodil owed Habiru two hours before the gun.
   Narrate mechanisms, not margins. (From §2; doctrine 6.)
